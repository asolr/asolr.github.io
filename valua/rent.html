Front End / Back End Ratios: If a homeowner has a mortgage, the front-end DTI ratio is usually calculated as housing expenses (such as mortgage payments, mortgage insurance, etc.) divided by gross income. In contrast, a back-end DTI calculates the percentage of gross income going towards other types of debt like credit card or car loans. Front End Ratio = PITI (monthly payment)/ gross monthly income Front End Ratio = Monthly Expenses / Monthly Income (is this also the LTV ratio?) Back End Ratio = (PITI + Monthly Debut) / Gross Monthly Income Back End Ratio = Total Monthly Debt Expense / Gross Monthly Income Debt to Income Ratio (DTI) Principal, Interest, Taxes, Insurance - PITI "points" to reduce overall interest Interest Rates Consider a 3% rate on a $100,000 loan.
